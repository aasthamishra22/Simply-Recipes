Poha

Poha is light, flavorful, and comforting. The flattened rice softens as it cooks, soaking up the gentle warmth of turmeric and the aroma of mustard seeds, curry leaves, and green chilies. Peanuts add a satisfying crunch, while a squeeze of lemon brings everything to life. It’s the kind of breakfast that feels familiar, filling, and quietly delicious.

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cooking Time

5 minutes

Servings

2

Tags: All Breakfast Dessert

Instructions

Step 1

Take 1 cup thick poha (flattened rice) and rinse it gently in a colander under running water. Let it sit and soften for 5–10 minutes. Meanwhile, chop onions, green chilies, and fresh coriander.

Step 2

Heat 1–2 tablespoons oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, curry leaves, and a pinch of hing (asafoetida). Let them crackle. Then add chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until onions turn translucent.

Step 3

Sprinkle turmeric and salt into the pan. Mix well. Add the softened poha and stir gently to coat it evenly. Cook for 2–3 minutes on low heat. Add roasted peanuts and a squeeze of lemon juice.

Step 4

Turn off the heat. Garnish with chopped coriander and optional grated coconut. Serve warm with a side of chai or curd.

Ingredients

1 cup thick Poha (flattened rice)

1 medium onion, finely chopped

1-2 green chillies, chopped

Oil

Mustard seeds

Cumin seeds

Curry Leaves

Turmeric Powder

0.25 cup roasted peanuts

0.5 Lemon

Corriander Leaves
